Space Lab Navigator

Information about NASA experiments conducted in space can be difficult to find and understand. This search engine simplifies access to that information in the form of diagrams. Simply search for the project you want to explore, and the artificial intelligence will provide you with the most relevant data and visualizations, ensuring that you don't miss any details. 


          

            

              The process starts when a user enters a search term into an input
              field. The system processes this query and searches NASA’s
              database to find experiments related to the user's input. This is
              the ouput of the "raw" data NASA's endpoint returns.
            

          

          
        

        

        

          

            After retrieving the initial data, an AI model powered by Hugging
            Face analyzes the results to extract deeper insights. For instance,
            upon searching for the experiment OSD-665, the model identifies its
            relevance to rats and discovers connections to other experiments,
            such as OSD-379. This enrichment process allows the system to
            provide users with a more comprehensive understanding of related
            studies and experimental contexts.
          


          
          

            Finally, step 1 concludes when another AI process gathers all the
            data and begins to retrieve the most important information,
            generating an output in JSON format. This output will be utilized by
            the presentation layer to create diagrams.
          

        

      

    


    

      

        

          With the output from the AI, the frontend begins to generate a tree
          diagram to visually represent the data. This tree diagram contains
          numerous other diagrams at its leaves, providing comprehensive visual
          representations of the data being obtained, including detailed
          relationships between experiments, the methodologies employed, and the
          observed behavioral outcomes.
